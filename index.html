About Me

I am a Machine Learning Research Assistant at Texas A&M University's Advanced Vision and Learning Lab. I am pursuing my M.S. in Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University, where I focus on developing advanced computer vision algorithms and deep learning models for complex data analysis. Prior to my graduate studies, I earned my B.E. in Electronics and Communication from Bangalore Institute of Technology. I have professional experience as an Android Security Software Engineer at Ittiam Systems Private Limited and as a Computer Vision Intern at EngineCAL.

My research interests lie in developing and applying advanced computer vision and machine learning techniques to solve complex problems in biomedical imaging and environmental health. I have published work on explainable AI for remote sensing image classification at IEEE IGARSS 2023 and on novel pooling techniques for improved image classification at the 2024 IEEE/CVF CVPR Vision for Agriculture Workshop. I am passionate about leveraging AI to advance biomedical imaging and computational biology.

Publications

Texture Analysis of Lung Cell Morphology After Nanoparticle Exposure

A. Mohan, T. Jefferis, C. Sayes, J. Peeples

SPIE Conference Proceedings, 2024 (Under Review)

Introduced a novel method for quantifying and analyzing cellular texture changes caused by nanoparticle exposure using advanced texture features like lacunarity and fractal dimension. We applied this technique to scanning electron microscopy (SEM) images of lung cells exposed to various nanoparticles, including nickel oxide, crystalline silica, isocyanate, and silver.

Spatial Transformer Network YOLO Model for Agricultural Object Detection

Y. Zambre, E. Rajkitkul, A. Mohan, J. Peeples

Under Review

Proposed an innovative integration of Spatial Transformer Networks (STNs) with the YOLO object detection model to address challenges in cluttered and partially occluded images.

Lacunarity Pooling Layers for Plant Image Classification using Texture Analysis

A. Mohan and J. Peeples

2024 IEEE/CVF Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Vision for Agriculture Workshop (Accepted)

Introduced a novel pooling technique to improve plant image classification by leveraging texture information.

Quantitative Analysis of Primary Attribution Explainable Artificial Intelligence Methods for Remote Sensing Image Classification

A. Mohan and J. Peeples

2023 IEEE International Geoscience and Remote Sensing Symposium (IGARSS)

Conducted a comprehensive analysis of XAI methods for improving interpretability in remote sensing applications.

Generation of Netlist from a Hand drawn Circuit through Image Processing and Machine Learning

Akshatha Mohan, A. Mohan, B Indushree, M Malavikaa, CP Narendra
